# 1-Pager Template & Guide

The 1-pager is the core sales artefact — what the salesperson sends before a call, shares during it, or follows up with after. It should be clean, specific to the company, and feel like it was written for them.

## Writing Notes

**The headline is the hardest part.** Spend time on it. It should feel like something a senior person at that company would read and think "that's us." Examples:

- ❌ "Shaping the Future of Tokenization" (generic, could be anyone)
- ✅ "BUIDL Proved the Concept. Help Write the Rules That Let It Scale." (BlackRock — specific, direct)
- ❌ "Join the Tokenization Working Groups" (sounds like a membership pitch)
- ✅ "Franklin Templeton Put a Fund on a Blockchain First. WG2 Is Where the Industry Follows." (specific to their business)
- ✅ "Canton Network Is Built for Tokenised Asset Interoperability. WG1 Is Where That Gets Its Rules." (specific, flattering, clear)

**The "Why Now" section should feel urgent, not manufactured.** Find real signals — a regulation being drafted, a market entry they just made, a competitor's recent move, a pilot being scaled. For tokenisation specifically, good urgency signals include: Basel III/IV treatment of tokenised assets being actively debated, MiCA implementation, IOSCO tokenisation principles, specific country CBDC launches, or a named competitor's tokenisation programme.

**"What You Get" — tailor the Market Visibility bullet.** The other four bullets are consistent across all 1-pagers. Examples of tailored Market Visibility lines:
- For an asset manager: *"Co-authorship at Singapore FinTech Festival alongside BIS and IMF — be the asset manager that helped design the regulatory framework your tokenised fund business depends on"*
- For a bank with tokenisation infrastructure: *"Co-authorship credit that positions [Bank]'s tokenisation programme as a contributor to global standards, not just a bilateral experiment"*
- For a custodian: *"Be the custodian associated with defining what digital asset safekeeping obligations should look like — not one that had to adapt to a framework written without you"*

**Language rules:**
- The initiative is now called "Tokenizing the Financial System" — not "Digital Money Working Groups"
- Say "implementation pathways" not "policy recommendations"
- Say the Oversight Committee "reviews" or "shapes" outputs — never "endorses"
- Never use internal participant tier names (Strategic Contributor, Active Contributor) — always write "As a working group sponsor, [Company] would:"
- The Oversight Committee is "chaired by BIS and IMF" — use this specific framing, it's more precise and credible than a general reference to central bank Governors

## Worked Example

```
---

# BlackRock × Tokenizing the Financial System

## BUIDL Proved the Concept. Help Write the Rules That Let It Scale.

BlackRock's BUIDL fund put over $500 million of tokenised US Treasuries on-chain
and demonstrated that institutional investment infrastructure can run on a blockchain.
The regulatory framework that lets that proof of concept become a global standard —
for fund distribution, custodian obligations, and investor access — is being written
right now. The institutions writing it are in this room. The question is whether
BlackRock helps write it or adapts to what others decide.

---

## Why Now

- Regulatory frameworks for tokenised investment vehicles are being developed across
  the US, EU, UK, and Singapore simultaneously — the next 18 months will determine
  how on-chain funds are treated by regulators in every market BlackRock operates in
- The Basel III/IV treatment of tokenised assets on bank balance sheets is actively
  unresolved — the answer will determine whether institutional counterparties can hold
  BUIDL at scale, or face punitive capital charges that limit adoption
- Franklin Templeton, Fidelity, and WisdomTree are all moving into tokenised fund
  products — the regulatory framework that emerges will create winners and losers
  based on who helped shape it

---

## The Initiative

Tokenizing the Financial System is a GFTN-convened, multi-stakeholder initiative
producing actionable implementation pathways on the most consequential questions in
the tokenisation of money, assets, and financial infrastructure. Three working groups
— on Interoperability, Regulation & Trust, and Financial Inclusion — develop practical
frameworks reviewed and shaped by an Oversight Committee chaired by BIS and IMF,
comprising Deputy Governors from MAS, Bank of England, ECB, and RBI.

Built on GFTN's Global Digital Assets Report (November 2025), outputs are published
in a consolidated report at Singapore FinTech Festival in November 2026, in conjunction
with BIS, IMF, Accion, and the Atlantic Council. Two in-person convenings take place
at Point Zero Forum and the Insights Forum.

---

## BlackRock's Role: Regulation & Trust (WG2)

WG2 is developing the implementation pathways for regulatory frameworks governing
tokenised investment vehicles — fund regulation for on-chain distribution, custodian
and safekeeping obligations for tokenised securities, investor accreditation rules,
and prudential capital treatment. These are the exact frameworks that will determine
how BUIDL — and every tokenised fund product that follows — is regulated globally.
BlackRock has more operational evidence on what these frameworks need to look like
in practice than any other participant in this working group.

**As a working group sponsor, BlackRock would:**
- Shape the regulatory framework for tokenised investment vehicles before it is set
  by regulators working without industry input — bringing BUIDL's implementation
  evidence directly into the WG2 problem statements
- Attend both in-person convenings alongside the central bank Deputy Governors and
  regulators whose decisions determine how tokenised funds are treated in their markets
- Be listed as a co-author on the consolidated report published at Singapore FinTech
  Festival — associating BlackRock's tokenisation leadership with the policy foundation
  that makes institutional adoption possible globally
- Build direct relationships with MAS, BOE, ECB, and RBI counterparts at a moment
  when their domestic tokenised asset regulatory frameworks are being actively developed

---

## What You Get

- **Policy Influence**: Direct input into recommendations presented to an Oversight
  Committee chaired by BIS and IMF — the implementation pathways shaping tokenised
  fund and securities regulation globally
- **Closed-door Access**: Two in-person convenings (Point Zero Forum + Insights Forum,
  2026) alongside senior regulators and institutional peers you won't find in the same
  room elsewhere
- **Market Visibility**: Co-authorship at Singapore FinTech Festival — be the asset
  manager that helped design the regulatory framework for tokenised investment vehicles,
  not the one that had to adapt to it
- **Peer Networks**: Direct relationships with the central bank counterparts, capital
  markets regulators, and institutional peers whose decisions shape the tokenised
  asset landscape BlackRock is building into
- **First-mover Positioning**: Be part of the initiative that sets the standard before
  the standard is set

---

## Next Step

A 20-minute call to understand your team's priorities around tokenised fund regulation
and cross-border regulatory harmonisation — and to explore whether a US$100,000
sponsorship commitment aligns with your 2026 budget. If there's appetite, we'll
introduce you to our WG2 programme orchestrator to walk through the specifics.
```
